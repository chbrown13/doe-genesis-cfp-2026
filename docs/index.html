Research System

PA-AKG — Provenance-Aware Agentic Knowledge Graph Systems

A provenance-first architecture integrating persistent knowledge graphs with multi-agent orchestration for accountable, cumulative AI-assisted scientific research

Scientific software is crucial for the DOE's research, integrating decades of expertise in numerical methods and HPC optimizations. While recent advances in AI, especially large language models, offer promising code assistance tools, they lack the trustworthiness necessary for high-performance computing applications, as they don't ensure traceability of changes to performance benchmarks or validation results. To resolve these issues, we propose the development of provenance-aware agentic knowledge graphs (PA-AKG) to enhance AI-assisted scientific software development, allowing for auditable code changes and improved reproducibility by linking AI recommendations to specific evidence and maintaining a comprehensive development history.

The key invariant: provenance enforcement is architectural, not post-hoc. Every agent handoff carries a provenance context object requiring downstream agents to inherit and extend the accumulated evidence chain. This transforms provenance from a reporting guideline into foundational research infrastructure, promoting trustworthy and accelerated scientific discovery powered by AI.

Knowledge Representation

Persistent research KG encoding problems, datasets, methods, constraints, and evidence as first-class entities with typed semantic relations. Hybrid property graph + vector index.

Automation & Extraction

Span-level evidence alignment linking each extracted claim to its source passage. Canonicalization auditing resolving entity references to canonical KG nodes across sessions.

Agentic Orchestration

Ranking, continuation, evaluation, and synthesis agents operating under provenance inheritance constraints. Structured hypothesis artifacts written back to the KG.

Evaluation framework (E1–E4): span-level alignment precision/recall (E1) · canonicalization consistency Cohen's κ (E2) · traceability completeness % (E3) · longitudinal stability score (E4)

DOE Genesis Mission Focus Area Framings

PA-AKG is submitted under three focus areas, each with a distinct framing of how the system advances that area’s specific scientific and technical goals.

[ARCHIVED] 11C
AI-Accelerated Science: Correlation to Understanding
Office of Science — Basic Energy Sciences (BES)
PA-AKG Framing

PA-AKG implements the closed-loop discovery cycle 11C calls for: a persistent KG encodes the current scientific model; agents compare incoming evidence against KG-encoded expectations; divergences trigger structured hypothesis generation with explicit provenance chains; confirmed hypotheses update the KG, enabling cumulative scientific progression across sessions.

[SUBMITTING!] 18E
Trustworthy AI for Scientific Software
Office of Science — ASCR
PA-AKG Framing

PA-AKG operationalizes the 18E requirements directly. Span-level evidence alignment and canonicalization auditing enforce provenance tracking as an architectural invariant—mandated by the Genesis Mission Executive Order. The E1–E4 evaluation framework delivers interpretable diagnostics and measurable reproducibility metrics, turning 18E’s trustworthiness requirements into quantitative targets.

[ARCHIVED] 19B
Hypothesis Generation from Multi-Modal Data
Office of Science — ASCR
PA-AKG Framing

PA-AKG synthesizes evidence across multi-modal scientific sources through a canonical KG substrate that links claims from literature, experimental data, and simulation outputs to the same entity nodes. Graph analysis identifies under-evidenced problems representing genuine knowledge gaps; synthesis agents generate structured, traceable hypotheses with provenance chains that DOE researchers can evaluate against primary evidence.
