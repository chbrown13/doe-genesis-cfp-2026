\small{\textbf{Topic:} B. Hypothesis Generation from Multi-Modal Data}

\subsubsection*{Project Summary / Abstract}

Generating novel, testable scientific hypotheses requires synthesizing knowledge from heterogeneous sources---scientific literature, experimental datasets, simulation outputs, and visual data---while maintaining traceable links between each hypothesis and the specific evidence supporting it. The Genesis Mission calls for AI systems that can perform exactly this synthesis: moving from passive information processing to active participation in the scientific method by identifying gaps in current knowledge and formulating testable hypotheses~\cite{EO14363}. Large language models can generate hypothesis-like text, but they cannot enforce \textit{span-level provenance}---the traceable chain from each generated claim back to the specific source passages, entities, and relations that license it~\cite{Ji2023HallucinationSurvey}. Without this infrastructure, AI-generated hypotheses are plausible-sounding conjectures, not verifiable scientific inferences that DOE researchers can act on.

The vision of this project is \textbf{provenance-aware agentic knowledge graphs (PA-AKG) as the infrastructure for traceable, multi-modal scientific hypothesis generation}. PA-AKG integrates three architectural layers that together enable AI to move from correlation to structured hypothesis. The \textit{knowledge representation layer} maintains a persistent research KG encoding scientific problems, datasets, methods, constraints, and evidence as first-class entities with explicit semantic relations~\cite{Hogan2021KnowledgeGraphs}---a queryable model of current scientific understanding that accumulates evidence across research sessions. The \textit{automation and extraction layer} ingests multi-modal scientific sources---text, experimental data, simulation outputs, and visual data---and produces \textit{span-level evidence alignment}: structured claim--evidence pairs linking each extracted claim to its specific supporting passage or data region, with \textit{canonicalization auditing} resolving entity references across all modalities to canonical KG nodes. The \textit{agentic orchestration layer} coordinates specialized agents: ranking agents prioritize research directions by evidence strength and tractability; continuation agents identify KG gaps representing genuine knowledge deficits; evaluation agents execute reproducible analyses; and synthesis agents generate structured hypothesis artifacts with explicit, auditable provenance chains. The key architectural invariant---every agent handoff inherits and extends the accumulated evidence chain---ensures provenance is enforced across the full multi-step reasoning pipeline.

PA-AKG achieves a decisive AI advantage in scientific hypothesis generation through three capabilities beyond the reach of current retrieval-augmented systems or human researchers working at the scale of modern scientific literature. First, \textbf{multi-modal synthesis}: the KG substrate links evidence across text, experimental data, simulation outputs, and visual data through canonical entity nodes, enabling agents to identify cross-modal correlations that require integrating evidence from sources no single researcher can read simultaneously. Second, \textbf{systematic gap identification}: graph analysis reveals under-evidenced problem nodes and missing semantic connections across all ingested modalities---the genuine knowledge deficits that 19B calls for AI to formulate into testable hypotheses---rather than re-deriving known correlations. Third, \textbf{traceable, falsifiable hypotheses}: every generated hypothesis is structured as a KG artifact with an explicit provenance chain linking each inferential step to its supporting evidence, enabling DOE researchers to evaluate, extend, or refute AI-generated hypotheses against primary data rather than trusting opaque model outputs on faith.

Our prior work directly establishes both the need and the feasibility of this approach~\cite{cusati2026fose, brown2025esem}. Primary validation uses the SE research corpus, with secondary validation in materials science to assess generalizability to DOE energy research domains. The interdisciplinary team spanning Virginia Tech (AI/NLP, software engineering) and Sandia National Laboratories (provenance infrastructure, DOE scientific data) is positioned to deliver a complete PA-AKG reference implementation within the one-year project scope. Known risks---cross-modal canonicalization failures when resolving entities across text and visual data, and hypothesis quality evaluation subjectivity---are directly addressed by the E2 canonicalization benchmarks and the expert-rated falsifiability and specificity criteria in RQ4.

\subsubsection*{Research Plan}

\paragraph{Introduction.}
DOE's 19B challenge calls for AI systems capable of synthesizing diverse scientific sources to identify knowledge gaps and formulate novel, testable hypotheses---a shift from AI as passive information processor to active participant in the scientific method. The research community has made substantial progress on retrieval-augmented generation~\cite{Lewis2020RAG} and graph-augmented reasoning~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG}, demonstrating that grounding LLM outputs in retrieved context improves factual accuracy. Yet these approaches share a fundamental limitation: they do not maintain persistent, structured knowledge that accumulates across research sessions, cannot systematically identify which problems are under-evidenced or contradictory, and do not enforce that generated hypotheses remain traceable to specific supporting evidence through multi-step reasoning chains.

The consequences of this gap are concrete. Consider a researcher using an agentic AI system to identify open problems in a scientific field. Even a sophisticated system with access to all relevant literature will, in the absence of persistent structured knowledge, re-derive the same correlations session after session, present known findings as discoveries, and generate hypotheses that combine evidence from different sources without tracking whether those sources actually support the combined inference. Reviewers cannot verify AI-generated hypotheses against primary evidence because the evidence chain is implicit in model weights, not explicit in system outputs. Trinkenreich et al.~\cite{Trinkenreich2025} warn of the epistemic risk of AI systems that ``generate content based on existing knowledge'' without provenance, leading to ``homogenization of research rather than groundbreaking discoveries.''

PA-AKG addresses these limitations through architectural integration: persistent KG, span-level provenance, and provenance-inheriting agents are not add-ons but design-time requirements. The gap analysis from prior work~\cite{cusati2026fose} identifies three independent gap categories---architectural capability, evaluation, and provenance/faithfulness---that form a cycle requiring simultaneous resolution. PA-AKG breaks this cycle by treating provenance enforcement as the architectural invariant from which persistence and evaluability follow.

\paragraph{Objectives.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Design and implement the PA-AKG three-layer architecture for multi-modal scientific hypothesis generation: knowledge representation layer, automation and extraction layer, agentic orchestration layer.
    \item Implement multi-modal ingestion pipelines producing span-level evidence alignment from scientific literature, experimental datasets, simulation outputs, and visual data, linking extracted claims to source passages, data regions, and canonical KG entities.
    \item Build canonicalization auditing that resolves entity references across heterogeneous source types to canonical KG nodes, enabling cross-modal scientific reasoning.
    \item Implement agentic orchestration with provenance inheritance constraints: ranking, continuation, evaluation, and synthesis agents producing structured hypothesis artifacts with auditable evidence chains.
    \item Evaluate hypothesis quality and provenance completeness along four quantitative axes (E1--E4) against RAG~\cite{Lewis2020RAG}, GraphRAG~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG}, and static snapshot baselines.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Research Questions.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1 (E1):} Does span-level evidence alignment produce claim--source links that match human expert annotations, measuring the fraction of AI-generated hypotheses with fully traceable provenance chains?
    \item \textbf{RQ2 (E2):} Does canonicalization auditing resolve entity references consistently with expert judgment across heterogeneous source types, enabling reliable cross-modal hypothesis generation?
    \item \textbf{RQ3 (E3):} Does PA-AKG's persistent KG maintain traceability completeness as the research corpus grows, compared to non-persistent RAG baselines?
    \item \textbf{RQ4:} Do provenance-grounded hypotheses generated by PA-AKG exhibit higher specificity, falsifiability, and expert-rated scientific quality than hypotheses generated by retrieval-only or graph-only approaches?
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Detailed Research Plan.}

\textit{Phase 1 (Months 1--3): Multi-Modal Knowledge Representation.}
We will design the research KG schema with entity types (\textsc{Problem}, \textsc{Dataset}, \textsc{Method}, \textsc{Constraint}, \textsc{Evidence}, \textsc{Hypothesis}) and typed semantic relations (\textsc{supports}, \textsc{contradicts}, \textsc{enables}, \textsc{requires}). Research problems are represented as tractable, scoped questions with identifiable assumptions, methods, and evaluation criteria, extracted from source documents with evidence spans anchored to source passages via character-level identifiers and quoted text. A hybrid symbolic--semantic design pairs a property graph store with a vector index, supporting both structured relational queries (``what problems cite this dataset?'') and approximate similarity search (``what methods are semantically similar to this approach?''). We will implement initial ingestion from the SE research corpus established in prior work~\cite{cusati2026fose, brown2025esem}, validate entity extraction accuracy against manually annotated ground-truth KGs, and establish baseline metrics for knowledge gap coverage and cross-source entity resolution.

\textit{Phase 2 (Months 4--6): Extraction Pipeline and Agentic Orchestration.}
We will implement the automation layer: structured prompting extracts claim--evidence pairs from text, data tables, and image captions; span-level alignment assigns character-level text references and region-level image references; canonicalization auditing clusters entity mentions across all modalities using embedding-based similarity, validates against KG constraints, and deduplicates to canonical nodes. The agentic orchestration layer implements four agent roles. \textit{Ranking agents} score research directions using a tractability composite: evidence strength (number of independent supporting spans), coverage (fraction of KG entities referenced), and novelty (semantic distance from existing \textsc{Hypothesis} nodes). \textit{Continuation agents} query the KG for under-evidenced \textsc{Problem} nodes and missing semantic connections, proposing targeted literature searches or experiments to fill identified gaps. \textit{Evaluation agents} invoke domain-specific analysis tools and write structured results back to the KG as new \textsc{Evidence} nodes. \textit{Synthesis agents} generate structured hypothesis artifacts with explicit provenance metadata and curate them to the KG as \textsc{Hypothesis} nodes. The provenance inheritance constraint is enforced at every agent handoff via a provenance context object that accumulates evidence chain references and blocks outputs that would discard inherited provenance~\cite{Asai2024SelfRAG}.

\textit{Phase 3 (Months 7--9): Hypothesis Quality Evaluation.}
We will evaluate along E1--E4. \textbf{E1} (span-level alignment): precision and recall against 500 human-annotated claim--evidence pairs from the SE corpus, stratified by claim type (factual, comparative, methodological). \textbf{E2} (canonicalization consistency): Cohen's $\kappa$ against expert entity clusters, with stratified analysis by entity type and source modality. \textbf{E3} (traceability completeness): fraction of KG nodes with complete provenance chains at corpus sizes of 100, 500, and 2,000 papers. \textbf{E4} (persistence): stability score across six corpus update cycles, measuring change detection accuracy against a versioned ground-truth KG. A hypothesis quality evaluation will assess whether PA-AKG hypotheses exhibit higher expert-rated specificity, falsifiability, and cross-source grounding compared to (B1) vector RAG without span alignment, (B2) GraphRAG~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG} without provenance enforcement, and (B3) LLM-only generation without retrieval. A secondary scientific domain will be evaluated to assess cross-domain generalizability of the extraction and canonicalization pipeline.
